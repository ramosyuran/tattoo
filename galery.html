Find the type of tattoo that we do, and some examples

Realism Tattoo

A tattoo that looks exactly like it would in the real world, A fine art skill, realistic tattoos done well look like photographs on the skin. Here, it's important to have a good photo reference and pick a skilled, well-known artist. Pay as much as you can for a tattoo of this kind - you get what you pay for, and cheap realism and portrait tattoos look whack.

Design Tattoo

Design is the oldest form of tattooing (after ancient tribal techniques). In the modern world, as we know it, tattooing began with this identifiable style. Solid black outlines, a limited colour palette, less detail and impeccable precision... traditional tattoos stand the test of time.

Color Tattoo

Currently very popular, colour tattoos mimic an experimental artistic process - using splashes and streaks of colour to give the impression of paint being used on canvas or paper.

Dotwork Tattoo

Detailed designs often cover large areas of the body such as the back or the arm. This tattooing style follows a very strict set of artistic rules - positioning, imagery used, direction, colour and more, all adhering to the ancient traditions.

Tribal Tattoo

Nearly always black in colour, often using symmetry and geometrical design. Sometimes un-detailed with block black lines, or like the tattoo below, utilising delicate detail and precision. A good artist will carefully design a piece to compliment the shape of the body, like this one.

Script Tattoo

Gothic, swirly, intricate fonts are popular but there's no limit to what font a script tattoo might utilise. A good script artist, like Boz De Niro, will create a completely custom tattoo for you, not use a downloadable font.
